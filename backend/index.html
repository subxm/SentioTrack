About SentioTrack

rocket_launch Project Overview & Purpose

target What is SentioTrack meant for?

SentioTrack is a real-time sentiment intelligence platform designed to bridge the gap between raw news coverage and actionable decision metrics.

By automatically scraping global articles and social posts for any given keyword, the platform filters noise, analyzes emotional tone, and compiles chronological metrics to plot moving-average sentiment trends.

build Why was it built?

Traditional sentiment analysis tools rely either on static dictionaries (which miss context and sarcasm) or heavy neural networks (which are computationally slow).

SentioTrack was built to demonstrate a hybrid **Dual-Engine Consensus** approach: utilizing VADER for baseline speed and a RoBERTa Transformer model for context-aware accuracy, weighting them together to achieve optimal precision.

Core Engine Architecture

1

Real-Time Data Acquisition & Preprocessing

When you search for a topic, our system automatically gathers the latest news articles and posts containing your keyword. The engine then filters the collection to remove duplicate stories, strips out junk web formatting, and organizes the raw text to prepare it for sentiment classification.

2

Rule-Based Lexicon Analyzer (VADER)

The first analysis model evaluates the text using a human-curated vocabulary list where positive and negative words have pre-assigned sentiment weights. By looking at grammatical rules, capitalization (e.g., words written in all caps), exclamation points, and intensifiers (like "extremely"), it calculates a swift baseline rating of the text's emotion.

3

Deep Learning Context Model (RoBERTa)

In parallel, we run a deep learning neural network model that has processed millions of social media posts to understand complex language patterns. Instead of just looking at individual words, this model evaluates how words relate to each other in context, allowing it to accurately detect sarcasm, local slang, and emotional nuances.

4

Smart Consensus Aggregation

To resolve disagreements between the two models—such as when a simple dictionary misses sarcasm that the deep learning model catches—the system aggregates their scores. The deep learning model is given higher weight because of its advanced context-awareness, producing a balanced consensus score.

5

Time-Series Trends & Keyword Heatmaps

All final results are saved securely in our database. The system then compiles the ratings chronologically to plot moving-average sentiment curves over time, and counts the most frequent positive and negative key terms to display interactive keyword clouds on your dashboard.

VADER vs. RoBERTa Engines

menu_book

VADER Engine

Valence Aware Dictionary & Sentiment Reasoner

VADER is a lexicon and rule-based sentiment analysis tool specifically attuned to sentiments expressed in social media. It combines a curated list of lexical features with general grammatical rules to determine sentiment intensity.

  • • Rule-Based Heuristics: Accounts for punctuation emphasis ("GREAT!!!"), capitalization ("GREAT"), degree modifiers ("extremely great"), and contrastive conjunctions ("but").
  • • Lexicon Dictionary: Utilizes a pre-scored word association list mapped by human evaluators.
  • • Ultra-Fast Scanning: Requires minimal processing power, making it incredibly fast.
speed Best for: High-velocity raw social streaming and instant baseline classification.
psychology

RoBERTa Engine

Robustly Optimized BERT Approach

RoBERTa is a large language transformer model trained on massive datasets. Instead of matching words directly, it parses entire sentences to recognize contextual structures and linguistic nuances.

  • • Contextual Embeddings: Analyzes how words interact with surrounding words rather than evaluating them in isolation.
  • • Sarcasm & Syntax: Detects complex grammatical structures, double negatives, ironies, and subtle contexts.
  • • Deep Learning Neural Model: Powered by transformer vectors to output highly accurate contextual probabilities.
verified Best for: Formal news editorial scanning, opinion pieces, and complex sentences.
